<p><p>The anti-infective vaccines market comprises several key types: </p><p>1. **Inactivated vaccines** use killed pathogens to stimulate an immune response, offering safety without the risk of disease. </p><p>2. **Conjugate vaccines** combine polysaccharides from pathogens with proteins to enhance immunogenicity, particularly in young children. </p><p>3. **Live/attenuated vaccines** contain weakened forms of the pathogen, provoking strong immune responses and long-lasting immunity. Each type plays a crucial role in preventing infections through targeted immunization strategies.</p></p>
